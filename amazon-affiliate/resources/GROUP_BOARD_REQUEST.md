# Pinterest Group Board Request Templates

## Overview
Group boards can significantly expand your reach on Pinterest. Use these templates to request to join relevant group boards.

---

## Finding Group Boards

### Where to Search:
1. **Pinterest Search:** Search "[your niche] group board"
2. **PinGroupie:** [pingroupie.com](https://pingroupie.com) - filter by niche and followers
3. **Competitor Research:** See which group boards successful accounts in your niche are part of
4. **Google Search:** "[niche] Pinterest group boards to join"

### What to Look For:
- Active boards (recently updated)
- Relevant to your niche
- Quality pins (not spammy)
- Clear join instructions
- Reasonable contributor count (not oversaturated)
- Good follower count (1,000+)

## Group Board Etiquette

### Do:
- Read and follow ALL board rules exactly
- Pin consistently (but not excessively)
- Pin your best content only
- Repin from other contributors
- Thank the board owner
- Stay on-topic for the board

### Don't:
- Spam multiple pins at once
- Pin off-topic content
- Only pin your own content (balance with repins)
- Pin low-quality or blurry images
- Ignore the board rules
- Request multiple times if rejected

---

## After Joining a Group Board

### First Week:
- [ ] Read rules thoroughly
- [ ] Study what other contributors pin
- [ ] Start with 1-2 pins per day maximum
- [ ] Engage with other pins (save, comment)

### Ongoing:
- [ ] Pin 2-5 times per week (or per rules)
- [ ] Track which group boards perform best
- [ ] Maintain high-quality standards
- [ ] Engage authentically

### If Rules Are Unclear:
- Message board owner for clarification
- Err on the side of pinning less, not more
- When in doubt, don't pin it

## Red Flags - Boards to Avoid

- Boards with spam/low-quality pins
- Boards with no engagement
- Boards requiring payment to join
- Boards with thousands of contributors (oversaturated)
- Boards that haven't been updated in months
- Boards with unclear or no rules
