Home Printing Guide

Recommended Paper

For the best results, we recommend 28-32lb (105-120gsm) bright white paper. This weight feels substantial and professional without being too thick for your home printer. Standard 20lb copy paper will work in a pinch, but the heavier weight makes a noticeable difference in how your journal feels in your hands.

Where to Buy

Look for "premium" or "presentation" paper at your local office supply store. HP Premium32, Hammermill Premium Color Copy, and Amazon Basics Premium are all great choices and typically cost $10-15 for 500 sheets.

Printer Settings

Essential Settings

  • Paper size: Letter or A4 (match your file)
  • Scaling: "Actual Size" or 100%
  • Quality: Best or High Quality
  • Color: Color (recommended)

Important: Do NOT

  • Select "Fit to Page" or "Shrink to Fit" — this will make everything slightly too small
  • Use "Draft" or "Economy" mode — colors will look washed out
  • Print on both sides unless your printer supports automatic duplex

Single-Sided vs. Double-Sided Printing

Single-Sided (Recommended)

  • Best for journaling — no bleed-through
  • Easier to rearrange pages later
  • Works with any pen or marker
  • Uses more paper but gives the best experience

Double-Sided (Paper Saver)

  • Use only with heavier paper (28lb+)
  • Enable "Flip on Long Edge" in printer settings
  • Test one page first for bleed-through
  • Stick with fine-tip pens (avoid markers)

Binding Your Journal

3-Hole Punch + Binder
Easiest option! Use a standard hole punch and a pretty binder. Easy to add or remove pages.
Spiral Binding
Take to a print shop for professional spiral binding. Lays completely flat! Usually $3-5.
Binder Clips
Quick and charming! Use two large binder clips on the left edge. Swap for pretty gold clips.
Comb Binding
Available at most office supply stores. Professional look and lays flat when open.
Ink-Saving Tip

This journal was intentionally designed to be ink-efficient. The soft sage and cream palette uses less ink than bold, saturated designs. If you'd like to save even more ink, most pages look beautiful printed in grayscale too!

Professional Print Shop Guide

For the most polished result, take your files to a local print shop. Places like FedEx Office, Staples, OfficeMax, and local print shops can produce beautiful results. Here's exactly what to tell them.

What to Tell the Print Shop

Simply hand them your USB drive or email the PDF and say:

"I'd like to print this PDF at actual size on 28-32lb bright white paper, single-sided, in full color, with highest quality. Then I'd like it spiral bound with a clear front cover and a cardstock back."

Recommended Paper Stock

Item Paper Stock Notes
Journal pages 28-32lb text / 80lb text Smooth, bright white. Resists bleed-through.
Journal cover 80-100lb cover / cardstock Thicker for a professional feel. Ask for a glossy or matte laminate for durability.
Affirmation cards 80-100lb cover / 65lb+ cardstock Thick and sturdy so cards feel substantial.
Wall art prints 80lb cover or matte photo paper Matte finish recommended for the botanical aesthetic.

Binding Options

Our Recommendation

  • Spiral binding with a clear front cover and black or white coil
  • Lays completely flat — perfect for journaling
  • Most print shops offer this
  • Typically $3-5 for binding

Other Options

  • Wire-O binding — more elegant, double-loop wire
  • Comb binding — affordable and widely available
  • Perfect binding — glued spine like a paperback book (less common, pricier)
  • Saddle stitch — stapled, best for thinner booklets

Money-Saving Tip

Ask about self-service color printers at FedEx or Staples — they're often 30-50% cheaper than full-service printing. You just upload your PDF to their machine and press print!

Printing the Affirmation Cards

The affirmation cards are formatted with crop marks for easy cutting. Print on 65lb+ cardstock for the best feel. Use a paper trimmer or craft knife with a ruler for clean, straight edges. For a special touch, round the corners using a corner punch (available at craft stores for about $5).

Digital Usage & Care

Prefer to go paperless? Your Calm & Bloom journal works beautifully as a digital journal on a tablet. Write directly on the pages using a stylus for a paper-like experience without the printing.

Using on a Tablet

iPad + Apple Pencil

The iPad is our top recommendation for digital journaling. The Apple Pencil feels incredibly natural and responsive. Import the PDF into GoodNotes or Notability and you'll have a beautiful, interactive journal you can write in, highlight, and annotate.

Android Tablet

Samsung Galaxy Tab with an S Pen or any Android tablet with a stylus works great. Use Xodo (free!) or Adobe Acrobat to open the PDF and write directly on the pages. Samsung Notes also supports PDF annotation.

Tips for Digital Journaling

  • Import the PDF as an editable document (not an image) so you can write on each page
  • Use a matte screen protector for a paper-like writing feel on your tablet
  • Set a pen color that complements the sage and lavender palette — try deep forest green or dusty rose
  • Duplicate pages you love (like the mood tracker) to use them again each week
  • Back up your annotated journal to cloud storage so you never lose your entries
  • Try journaling at the same time each day to build a calming routine
