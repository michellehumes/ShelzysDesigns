## üé® Brand Aesthetic Guidelines

### **Color Palette**:
- Soft neutrals (cream, beige, soft gray)
- Accent colors: blush pink, sage green, navy
- Avoid harsh/bright colors
- Natural wood tones

### **Lighting**:
- Natural light preferred (golden hour, soft window light)
- Bright and airy (not dark or moody)
- Minimal shadows
- Avoid direct harsh sunlight

### **Composition**:
- Rule of thirds
- Clean backgrounds (not cluttered)
- Bottles should be prominent but not isolated
- Include hands/people for scale and emotion

### **Style References**:
- Modern villa aesthetic (think: Italian coast, Santorini)
- Elevated yet attainable
- Instagram-worthy but authentic
- NOT overly staged or fake

### **Similar Brands for Inspiration**:
- Anthropologie
- Free People (lifestyle shots)
- Jenni Kayne (home aesthetic)
- The Knot / Brides (wedding inspiration)

## üìè Technical Specifications

### Image Requirements:
- **Resolution**: Minimum 2000px on longest side
- **Format**: JPEG (for web) + RAW files (for editing)
- **Orientation**: Mix of landscape (homepage), square (Instagram), vertical (product pages)
- **File Size**: Under 500KB after optimization (use TinyPNG)
- **Color Mode**: sRGB

### Deliverables Per Scenario:
- 15-20 edited final images
- 5-10 alternate angles/compositions
- All RAW files for future editing

**Total**: 60+ edited images across all 3 scenarios

## üì¶ Bottle Specifications for Shoot

### What Photographer Needs:
- 10-15 physical water bottles with custom names
- Mix of colors: Navy, Gray, Black, Blush, Sage Green
- Names to use: "Sarah", "Emily", "Lauren", "Jessica", "Rachel", "Ashley", "Taylor"

### Prep Before Shoot:
1. Clean bottles thoroughly (no fingerprints, smudges)
2. Bring extra bottles as backups
3. Bring lint roller for removing dust
4. Microfiber cloths for quick cleaning between shots
